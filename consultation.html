Consultations

Two forms of consultation are available


  1. 1 - Home consultation at the place where the cat or cats are kept, Greater Cape Town area and surrounding towns, South Africa.

  2. 2 - Email consultations



Although initial contact may be via phone, WhatsApp or Facebook messenger, these are not as easy to manage or control for multiple conversations.


Home Consultations


A home consultation typically lasts between 1 and 2 hours and is arranged over weekends and holidays so as to involve the whole family.

In a consultation we look at what the issue is, and how the family would like to see the cats behave differently.

The history of each of the cats is discussed, their personalities, likes, dislikes and relationships with other pets and family members. There is a discussion on how their history, personality and experiences could lead to their behaviour.

The environment and resources play a large role in behaviour; we look at what is currently in place and what options there are for making changes within budget limits and taking into consideration rental agreements and estate complex regulations.

Other options are discussed and a plan of action is proposed, based on your lifestyle and ability to manage changes, taking into account the ability of the cats to adapt to change. Then it is up to the family to work through the suggestions, find what works and what doesnâ€™t, and provide feedback or ask for more or different ideas.

At times, I will recommend another practitioner, therapist, or a vet visit. These are recommendations and should be considered by the family.

Email Consultations


Email consultations follow the same general format as home consultations, except that I cannot meet the cats, see their personalities or behaviour, or see your environment; I rely on you to tell me about everything.

After the initial contact, which gives a summery of the concerns, I need information about your cats and the environment. Usually this takes the form of questions and answers, followed by more questions and answers interspersed with thoughts and comments, as I begin to understand the cats, the environment, the family and the behaviour.

Recommendations are not as geared to the family as for a home consultation; they are more general and need to be interpreted by the family in the best way to fit their situation for maximum benefit.

Consultation Fees


The standard fee for a home consultation is R500, plus possible additional travel costs, depending on where the consultation takes place. Discounted rates are available for pensioners, known rescue centres, and recently-adopted cats.

Fees for email consultations are variable, and will be discussed and agreed at the beginning of the session.
